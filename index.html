Various stats about the players in the data set

These days a lot of statistics about each and every single batsmen are being calculated. Some of these most important statistcs that basically define the player are included in this data set.
Mat: This column gives information on total number of matches a particular individ- ual has played in that particular season. Since a team has to play atleast 14 matches , a players who has played 14 or above can be considered a good player since their team didn’t dropped him for a single match.
NO: This column gives information on how many times a player has not been out in an entire innings though he entered the crease.
Runs: This column shows the total number of runs scored by a particular individual.
HS: This column gives the highest score that a particular individual has scored in that season.
Avg: This column gives the average runs scored by a payer in that season.
BR: This column gives the total number of balls a particular player has faced in that season.
SR: This column gives strike rate of a player in that season. This is calculated using the other two columns, Runs and BF. Strike Rate = (Runs/BF(Balls Faced)) x 100
100 : This column gives the number of centuries scored by a player. This information shows that a player has the capabilities to win a match for his team as a one man army, because scoring 100 in t20 is really tough , this information shows that he can take the responsibility to carry the team for victory.
50: This column gives the number of half-centuries scored by a player.
4’s: This column gives the number of 4’s scored by a player.
6’s: This column gives the number of 6’s scored by a player. This information can be used to identify an explosive and aggressive batsman who has the power to hit maximums when they are most needed for the team.



Visualization Using Gephi

ABOUT GEPHI:

Gephi is an open-source and free software application used for visualizing and exploring complex networks and graphs. It was first released in 2008 and is now widely used in various fields, including social sciences, biology, and computer science. Gephi provides a user-friendly and interactive interface that allows users to import, manipulate, and visualize data in the form of nodes and edges. Nodes represent the entities in the network, while edges represent the connections or relationships between them. The data can be imported from various sources such as CSV files, spreadsheets, and databases.
